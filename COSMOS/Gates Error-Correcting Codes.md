# Gates Error-Correcting Codes

**Source**: S. James Gates Jr., Charles Doran, Michael Faux et al., "Adinkras: A Graphical Technology for Supersymmetric Representation Theory," *Physical Review D* 71:065002, 2005. Further developed in Gates, "Symbols of Power," *Physics World*, 2010. Doran, Faux, Gates et al., *Advances in Theoretical and Mathematical Physics* 22(5):1-56, 2018 (arXiv:0806.0051).

## Finding

While developing Adinkras -- graphical representations of supersymmetry algebras that encode the relationships between bosons and fermions -- Gates and collaborators discovered that certain doubly-even self-dual linear binary block codes, structurally identical to error-correcting codes used in information technology (related to Hamming and Golay codes), appear embedded in the mathematical structure of supersymmetry (SUSY) equations.

These are not analogies or metaphors. The same algebraic objects that correct transmission errors in digital communication appear as necessary components of the mathematical consistency conditions for supersymmetric field theories.

Gates has stated explicitly that this does not support the simulation hypothesis -- that inference is not falsifiable and therefore not scientific.

**Non-fabrication** -- The simulation hypothesis that some commentators have drawn from this result is precisely the kind of fabrication the finding does not support. The mathematics is real; the metaphysical interpretation is speculation.
