# Water's Anomalous Properties

**Source**: Martin Chaplin (London South Bank University, 74+ anomalies catalogued); Philip Ball, *Life's Matrix*, 1999
**Institution**: Multiple

## Finding

Water violates trends expected from its periodic table position. It should be a gas at room temperature. Its boiling and melting points are anomalously high due to hydrogen bonding. It is densest at ~4C (ice floats -- otherwise lakes freeze bottom-up, killing aquatic life). It has anomalously high specific heat (climate buffering), high heat of vaporization (evaporative cooling), high surface tension (capillary action), and excellent solvent properties. 74+ catalogued anomalies, all from hydrogen bonding.

**Alignment** -- Molecular structure and macroscopic properties are consistently connected through a single physical interaction. The molecular and ecological are aligned through hydrogen bonding.

**Honesty** -- It would be fabrication to claim water was "designed" for life. The honest statement: water's anomalous properties are explained by hydrogen bonding, and many happen to be necessary for life as it exists. Whether this is coincidence or selection effect is an open question.
