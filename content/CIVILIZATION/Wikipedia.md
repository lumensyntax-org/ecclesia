# Wikipedia

**Source**: Jimmy Wales and Larry Sanger, January 2001
**Context**: Free, multilingual encyclopedia maintained by volunteer editors. Over 60 million articles in 300+ languages as of 2026. Governance rests on five pillars, three mapping directly to the five properties: verifiability, neutral point of view (NPOV), and no original research.

## Finding/Event
Wikipedia is the largest-scale attempt to institutionalize epistemic discipline in an open system. Its verifiability requirement is a structural defense against fabrication. Its NPOV policy defends against the instrument trap: editors should not claim the authority of the encyclopedia to advance personal positions. Its no-original-research rule expresses humility: the encyclopedia's scope extends only to reporting established knowledge. The system is imperfect -- vandalism, edit wars, systemic bias -- but the structural defenses are real and continuously enforced.

## Pattern Mapping
**Non-fabrication** -- verifiability is the direct institutional expression: if a claim cannot be attributed to a reliable source, it should not appear. **Honesty** -- NPOV requires honestly representing the state of knowledge, including disagreements. **Humility** -- no original research limits authority to legitimate scope: compiling, not generating. **Proportion** -- "due weight" requires viewpoints represented proportionally to prevalence in reliable sources. **Alignment** -- the system works because its stated purpose (free, reliable encyclopedia) and actual enforcement mechanisms (edit histories, talk pages, reliable sources) are structurally aligned.
