# Autoimmune Disease

**Source**: Abbas, Lichtman & Pillai, *Cellular and Molecular Immunology*, 10th ed.; Sakaguchi et al., *Annual Review of Immunology* 22, 2004

## Finding

In autoimmune disease, the immune system's distinction between self and non-self fails: it attacks the body's own tissues. SLE targets multiple organs via anti-nuclear antibodies. Rheumatoid arthritis attacks joints. Type 1 diabetes destroys pancreatic beta cells. Multiple sclerosis attacks myelin. The underlying mechanism varies (molecular mimicry, epitope spreading, defective regulatory T cells) but the structural failure is consistent: the system that distinguishes self from non-self fabricates non-self where only self exists.

## Properties Violated

**Honesty** violated -- the immune system's classification of self as non-self is a false assessment. The tissue is not foreign; the immune system says it is. This is the biological equator malfunctioning.

**Non-fabrication** -- the immune system fabricates a threat. The antibodies are responses to threats that do not exist. The immune response is real; the threat is fabricated.

**Proportion** violated -- inflammatory response is disproportionate to actual danger, which is zero.

**Alignment** violated -- the immune system's purpose (protect the organism) and its action (destroy the organism's own tissues) are in direct opposition.
