# Truth and Reconciliation Commissions

**Source**: South Africa TRC, 1995; Desmond Tutu, *No Future Without Forgiveness*, 1999; Hayner, *Unspeakable Truths*, 3rd ed., 2018

## Finding

South Africa's TRC offered conditional amnesty for full disclosure -- amnesty for honesty, not for silence. The TRC heard testimony from approximately 21,000 victims and received 7,112 amnesty applications, granting 1,500 (Commission final report, 1998/2003). Adapted in Canada (2008-2015), Rwanda (Gacaca courts, 2001-2012), Colombia (2017-present). The structural choice: when prosecuting all offenders is impossible, truth becomes the price of freedom.

## Pattern Mapping

**Honesty** -- Honesty as the central transaction of transitional justice. Tell the full truth and avoid prosecution; lie and face it. In systemic atrocity, the truth itself -- publicly stated, officially recorded -- may be more valuable than individual punishment.

**Alignment** -- Mechanism suited to actual conditions. Conventional criminal justice aligns purpose with mechanism for ordinary crime; the TRC adapted alignment for extraordinary circumstances.

**Non-fabrication** -- Apartheid depended on fabrication: fabricated racial categories, fabricated justifications, fabricated narratives of benevolence. The TRC replaced the official lie with testified truth.
