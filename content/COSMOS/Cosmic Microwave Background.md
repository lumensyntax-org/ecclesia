# Cosmic Microwave Background

**Source**: Arno Penzias & Robert Wilson, 1965 (Nobel 1978); George Smoot & John Mather, COBE (Nobel 2006); Planck Collaboration, 2018
**Institution**: Bell Labs; NASA; ESA

## Finding

The Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) is thermal radiation filling the entire observable universe at 2.7255 +/- 0.0006 K, emitted ~380,000 years after the Big Bang. It is the most perfect blackbody spectrum ever measured -- COBE's FIRAS instrument found deviations of less than 50 parts per million. Tiny temperature fluctuations (~1 part in 100,000) map the density variations in the early universe that became galaxies, galaxy clusters, and the cosmic web.

## Pattern Mapping

**Honesty** -- The CMB is the universe's unedited record of its own origin. It cannot be falsified, altered, or rewritten. Every photon carries information about the state of the universe 13.8 billion years ago, undistorted to extraordinary precision.

**Alignment** -- The temperature fluctuations in the CMB and the large-scale structure of the universe today are the same structure at different epochs. The early universe and the late universe are aligned across 13.8 billion years.

**Proportion** -- The fluctuations are tiny (1 part in 100,000) yet sufficient to seed all cosmic structure. Minimal initial conditions, amplified proportionally by gravity over billions of years.
