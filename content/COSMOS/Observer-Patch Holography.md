# Observer-Patch Holography (OPH)

**Source**: Bernhard Mueller, "Observers are All You Need: How Observer-Synchronization Creates All of Physics," March 30, 2026. Security researcher turned theoretical physicist.
**Publication**: Medium article with referenced technical papers. NOT peer-reviewed. Preprint-level.

## Finding

OPH proposes that all of physics emerges from one principle: no observer ever experiences the whole world at once. Each observer has a finite holographic patch (a region of a 2-sphere S²). When patches overlap, they must agree on shared observables. From this constraint and a small number of input constants, Mueller claims to derive: 3+1D Lorentzian spacetime (from S² conformal symmetry = Lorentz group), general relativity (from modular flow + entropy stationarity), the Standard Model gauge group SU(3)×SU(2)×U(1) (from patch-gluing fusion rules), particle masses matching measured values for W, Z, Higgs, and top quark to high precision, the Born rule for quantum probabilities (from overlap consistency), and a resolution of the measurement problem (observers built in from the start). String theory emerges as effective description, not fundamental.
