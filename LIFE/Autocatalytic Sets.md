# Autocatalytic Sets

**Source**: Stuart Kauffman, *The Origins of Order*, 1993; Wim Hordijk & Mike Steel, *Journal of Theoretical Biology* 227, 2004
**Institution**: Santa Fe Institute; University of Canterbury

## Finding

Kauffman proposed that life originated through collectively autocatalytic sets -- molecular networks where each molecule's formation is catalyzed by at least one other molecule in the set. No single molecule replicates itself; the set as a whole is self-sustaining. Above a critical threshold of molecular diversity, the probability of an autocatalytic set approaches 1. Hordijk and Steel formalized this as RAF theory (Reflexively Autocatalytic and Food-generated sets).

## Pattern Mapping

**Alignment** -- The network's collective function (self-sustenance) emerges from individual catalytic actions. No molecule needs to "know" the whole; each does its part. Alignment between individual action and collective outcome is structural, not designed.

**Humility** -- The framework is explicitly humble about requirements for life's origin. It does not require a miracle molecule. It requires only sufficient molecular diversity for catalytic closure to become statistically inevitable. The threshold is a property of chemistry.

## Status

Established theoretical chemistry/mathematical biology. Experimental demonstrations partial (Ashkenasy et al., *PNAS*, 2004). The theory has not replaced the RNA World; the two may be complementary. The mapping to the five properties is this project's structural interpretation.
