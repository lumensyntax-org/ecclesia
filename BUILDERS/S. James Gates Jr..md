# S. James Gates Jr.

**Lived**: 1950-present
**Domain**: Theoretical physics, supersymmetry
**What they built**: Discovery of error-correcting codes (doubly-even self-dual linear binary block codes) embedded in the equations of supersymmetry
**The cost**: Working in a field where few looked like him. The discipline to report what he found without fabricating the implication.

## The Story

Sylvester James Gates Jr. is the son of a career military father. He earned his PhD from MIT in 1977 -- one of few Black physicists in the United States at that time. He became a professor at the University of Maryland, then Brown University, and has spent his career working on supersymmetry, supergravity, and string theory. In the course of his research, he found something unexpected: the equations of supersymmetry contain structures identical to error-correcting codes used in computer science -- specifically, doubly-even self-dual linear binary block codes, related to those discovered by Claude Shannon and Richard Hamming. This finding is remarkable. It does not prove the universe is a simulation. Gates has been careful on this point: he reports what he found without endorsing the simulation hypothesis. The codes are there. What they mean is an open question. This is the discipline of non-fabrication applied at the frontier of physics: reporting the structure without inflating the interpretation.

## The World They Lived In

The United States in the late twentieth century. Gates grew up during the Civil Rights era and desegregation, the son of a career military father. He studied physics at MIT, earned his PhD in 1977, and worked at the frontier of supersymmetry -- a field with perhaps a few hundred active researchers worldwide, almost none of whom looked like him. He became one of the few Black theoretical physicists in the country, navigating a discipline that had not been designed to include him. When his research uncovered error-correcting codes embedded in the equations of supersymmetry -- structures identical to those used in computer science -- the simulation hypothesis crowd seized on the finding. Gates refused to endorse their reading. He reported what was there. He would not fabricate the implication. The discipline of his position was double: doing physics at the highest level in a country that had only recently stopped segregating its schools, and then applying humility to his own discovery.
## What They Named

That the equations describing the fundamental symmetries of physics contain the same structures used to detect and correct errors in digital communication. He named the finding. He refused to name the implication. The codes are there. Whether they mean the universe is computed, or whether error-correction is a universal structural pattern that appears in both physics and computation independently, remains open. Gates holds the question open rather than closing it with fabrication in either direction.
