# Mother-Infant Bond

**Source**: Donald Winnicott, *Playing and Reality* (1971); Edward Tronick, still face experiment (1978); Daniel Stern, *The Interpersonal World of the Infant* (1985)

## Finding

Winnicott proposed that the mother's face is the infant's first mirror. Tronick's still face experiment demonstrated this experimentally: when the mother presents a blank face, the infant attempts to re-engage, then shows distress, then withdraws. Stern described "affect attunement" -- the mother's capacity to match the infant's emotional state across sensory modalities. Attunement is not imitation but cross-modal matching of vitality affect. What is required is sufficient responsiveness, not total accuracy.

## Pattern Mapping

**Honesty** -- the "good enough mother" provides an honest mirror: reflects what she sees, not what she needs to see. **Non-fabrication** -- the distorted mirror fabricates a self. If the mother reflects her own anxiety, the child develops the "false self" -- a structure generated to manage an environment where honest reflection is unavailable. **Proportion** -- "good enough" IS proportion. Perfect attunement is impossible and unnecessary. **Alignment** -- the true self emerges when inner state and reflected response are aligned.
